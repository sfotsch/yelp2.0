John R.

Cheery, cozy, unpretentious cafe serving delicious breakfast at criminally underpriced rates. We visited this place on a trip to South Bend, and it was one of those culinary diamonds in the rough located in a pretty nondescript setting. The staff is pleasant without being too chatty, and very efficient. Even though we went on a Sunday morning, we had no problem getting a huge corner booth (there were 7 of us) without a waiting time. I'm guessing the place was not as full as it normally is, since the college students must be on their summer vacations.

Shawn F.

A small breakfast spot in a college housing area, I'm into it, even though there were a few times when I felt old as hell! This cozy cafe is a great stop for a quick bite and a study session. There's seating both inside and outside, but I opted to dine inside so I could use my laptop without the sunny glare. The menu is written on the wall and has tons of great options for breakfast and lunch. I went with the build your bfast sandwich option.

Jenna W.

was in Southbend this weekend for a wedding, and needed to find a place where I could get food and coffee and find a quiet corner to study. Thankfully, Cafe at the Overlook served all three purposes! I loved the place so much I even took my parents back later that weekend after all the wedding festivities had ended! It definitely beat paying for overpriced food at the bistro next door. I tried the Jerk Panini and also the BBQ Pork sandwich. The first with a side of soup and the second with a side of salad. Both were delicious options and super reasonably priced for a filling meal ($10 with the add-on side). The pulled pork sandwich was pretty standard, nothing spectacular. However, I found the Jerk Panini to be very unique. It is served with mango mayo and banana smear which really gives it a tropical flavor! It also doesn't hurt that it comes with a healthy serving of bacon as well. :)
